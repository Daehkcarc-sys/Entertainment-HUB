Anime Terminology

Genres and Demographics
Shonen (少年)
Anime targeted towards teenage boys, typically featuring action, adventure, and coming-of-age themes. Popular examples include "Naruto," "One Piece," and "Dragon Ball."
Shojo (少女)
Anime aimed at teenage girls, often focusing on romance, relationships, and emotional growth. Examples include "Fruits Basket," "Sailor Moon," and "Ouran High School Host Club."
Seinen (青年)
Anime for adult men, usually containing more mature themes, complex characters, and sophisticated storylines. "Berserk," "Monster," and "Vinland Saga" are notable examples.
Josei (女性)
Anime for adult women, featuring realistic romance and slice-of-life stories with mature themes. Examples include "Nodame Cantabile," "Nana," and "Paradise Kiss."
Common Tropes & Settings
Isekai (異世界)
A genre where characters are transported to or reborn in another world, often a fantasy universe. Examples include "Re:Zero," "Sword Art Online," and "That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime."
Mecha (メカ)
Anime featuring large robots or machines piloted by humans. Famous examples are "Gundam," "Neon Genesis Evangelion," and "Gurren Lagann."
Slice of Life (日常系)
Anime focusing on everyday experiences and relationships rather than fantastic adventures. "K-On!," "Barakamon," and "Non Non Biyori" are popular examples.
Harem
A genre where multiple characters are romantically interested in the protagonist. Examples include "The Quintessential Quintuplets," "Love Hina," and "Monster Musume."
Industry Terms
Cour
A unit of TV broadcasting in Japan that refers to a three-month or one-season block of programming. A one-cour anime typically has 10-13 episodes.
OVA/OAV
Original Video Animation/Original Animation Video. Anime released directly to home video formats without prior TV broadcast or theatrical release.
Seiyuu (声優)
Voice actors who perform the voices for anime characters. Many become celebrities in Japan and have dedicated fan followings.
Sakuga
A term referring to notably high-quality animation sequences in anime that stand out for their fluid motion and detailed artwork.
